Events

a. Quest for Knowledge: Oral Presentation

    • Oral presentations at InGARSS 2024 cover a wide range of topics, including but not limited to:
    •  ○   Remote sensing technologies and methodologies
    •  ○   Satellite and aerial imaging systems
    •  ○   Data acquisition, processing, and analysis techniques
    •  ○   Applications of remote sensing in environmental monitoring and management
    •  ○   Land use and land cover mapping
    •  ○  Climate change monitoring and assessment
    •  ○  Disaster management and response using remote sensing
    •  ○  Remote sensing for agriculture, forestry, and natural resource management
    •  ○  Urban remote sensing for smart cities
    •  ○   Remote sensing for oceanography and hydrology

Presenters at InGARSS 2024 oral sessions typically share their latest research findings, innovative methodologies, case studies, and practical applications of remote sensing technologies. These presentations provide a platform for researchers, scientists, engineers, and practitioners from around the world to exchange knowledge, collaborate on cutting-edge research, and advance the field of geoscience and remote sensing.

b. Exploration Track: Poster Presentation

Poster presentations at InGARSS 2024 provide an opportunity for participants to present their research findings, projects, methodologies, and applications in a concise and visually appealing manner.

    • Here's what typically happens during an InGARSS 2024 poster presentation:
    •  ○   Preparation: Presenters create a poster summarizing their research, including key findings, methodologies, and results. The poster usually includes text, figures, tables, and images to effectively communicate the research.
    •  ○   Display: Presenters set up their posters in a designated area of the conference venue. Each presenter is usually assigned a specific time slot during which they stand by their poster to discuss their work with conference attendees.
    •  ○   Interaction: Conference attendees visit the poster area to view the displays and engage in discussions with presenters. Presenters explain their research, answer questions, and provide additional information to interested attendees.
    •  ○   Networking: Poster presentations offer opportunities for networking and collaboration among researchers with similar interests. Attendees may exchange contact information, discuss potential collaborations, or share insights and feedback on each other's work.
    •  ○   Evaluation: In some cases, poster presentations may be evaluated by judges or reviewers as part of a poster competition. Presenters may receive feedback on their presentations and have the chance to win awards or recognition for their work.

c. Invited Special Sessions

The sessions are typically feature presentations by invited speakers who are experts in their respective fields, as well as contributed papers related to the session theme. Invited special sessions serve to highlight cutting-edge research, foster discussion and collaboration among researchers, and provide attendees with insights into new developments and challenges in the field.

    •  ○   Focused Topics: Invited special sessions typically focus on specific topics or themes within the broader field of geoscience and remote sensing. These topics may encompass emerging technologies, novel methodologies, interdisciplinary research areas, or applications in specific domains such as environmental monitoring, disaster management, or urban planning.
    •  ○   High-Quality Presentations: Invited speakers are often recognized experts or leaders in their fields, selected for their expertise and contributions to the topic of the session. As such, presentations in invited special sessions are expected to feature high-quality research, innovative approaches, and significant contributions to the field.
    •  ○   In-depth Discussions: Invited special sessions provide a forum for in-depth discussions and exchange of ideas among participants. Presenters may engage with attendees through presentations, panel discussions, or Q&A sessions, encouraging dialogue and collaboration on the session topic.
    •  ○   Networking Opportunities: Attendees of invited special sessions have the opportunity to network with fellow researchers, industry professionals, and experts in the field. These sessions often attract a diverse audience interested in the session topic, facilitating connections and collaborations among participants.
    •  ○  Visibility and Recognition: Invited special sessions offer visibility and recognition for both the organizers and presenters, showcasing their expertise and contributions to the field of geoscience and remote sensing. Presenters may gain exposure to a wider audience within the conference community, leading to potential collaborations, partnerships, or opportunities for further research.

d. Tutorial sessions – Hands-on training

Hands-on training is essential for holistic learning and skill development, providing participants with valuable experiences, practical skills, and confidence needed for success in their academic and professional endeavours. Hands-on training is of paramount importance for several reasons:

    •  ○   Active Learning: Hands-on training engages participants actively in the learning process. It allows them to apply theoretical knowledge in practical scenarios, reinforcing concepts and enhancing understanding.
    •  ○   Skill Development: Practical experience enables individuals to develop and refine skills relevant to their field of study or profession. Whether it's technical skills, problem-solving abilities, or critical thinking, hands-on training provides opportunities for skill acquisition and mastery.
    •  ○   Real-World Application: Hands-on training bridges the gap between theory and practice by allowing participants to experience real-world scenarios. This practical exposure helps learners understand how concepts and skills are applied in professional settings, preparing them for future challenges and opportunities.
    •  ○  Retention and Recall: Research has shown that active learning methods, such as hands-on training, improve retention and recall of information. By actively engaging with the material through practical exercises and experiences, participants are more likely to remember and apply what they have learnt.
    •  ○   Confidence Building: Hands-on training builds confidence and self-efficacy. As participants gain hands-on experience and see tangible results, they become more confident in their abilities and more willing to take on new challenges.
    •  ○   Problem-Solving Skills: Practical training often involves troubleshooting and problem-solving, which are essential skills in any field. Through hands-on exercises, participants learn to identify issues, analyze problems, and develop effective solutions, enhancing their problem-solving capabilities.
    •  ○   Teamwork and Collaboration: Many hands-on training activities involve teamwork and collaboration, fostering interpersonal skills and teamwork abilities. Participants learn to communicate effectively, work cooperatively with others, and leverage collective strengths to achieve common goals.
    •  ○   Adaptability and Flexibility: Practical training exposes participants to diverse situations and scenarios, helping them develop adaptability and flexibility. By encountering various challenges and working through different scenarios, learners become more resilient and adaptable to change.

e. Case Studies – Industry Track

Case Studies - Industry Track is an event focused on showcasing real-world applications, challenges, and successes within specific industries through the lens of case studies. This event provides a platform for industry professionals, experts, and researchers to share insights, best practices, and lessons learned from practical experiences in their respective fields.

f.  PhD Pioneers: Trailblazing Ideas Symposium

The "PhD Pioneers: Trailblazing Ideas Symposium" is an event that celebrates the innovative spirit and groundbreaking ideas of doctoral students. In this symposium, doctoral candidates from various fields come together to showcase their research and present their pioneering ideas that have the potential to shape the future.

This event provides a platform for doctoral students to share their innovative projects, cutting-edge research, and novel concepts with peers, academics, industry professionals, and the wider community. From advanced technologies to revolutionary theories, attendees will witness the diverse range of ideas being explored by the next generation of thought leaders.

The event fosters collaboration, inspires creativity, and encourages interdisciplinary exchange as participants explore the frontiers of knowledge across different disciplines. Through presentations, posters, demonstrations, and interactive discussions, attendees have the opportunity to engage with emerging ideas, provide feedback, and spark new connections that could lead to future collaborations or transformative discoveries.

Join us as we celebrate the visionaries, innovators, and change-makers of tomorrow at the event, where groundbreaking ideas take centre stage and the future begins to unfold.

g. Mentor’s Talk

The "Mentor's Talk" is a special session designed to provide guidance, insights, and mentorship to students, early-career professionals, and aspiring researchers. In this session, experienced mentors and industry leaders share their knowledge, expertise, and advice on navigating the academic and professional landscape, overcoming challenges, and achieving success in the field of geoscience and remote sensing.

The format of "Mentor's Talk" typically involves mentor-led presentations, panel discussions, or one-on-one mentoring sessions, where mentors share their stories, lessons learned, and strategies for success. These talks may cover a wide range of topics, including career development, leadership skills, goal setting, overcoming challenges, work-life balance, and personal growth.

h. Women In Engineering (WIE)

"Women in Engineering" is an event dedicated to celebrating and empowering women in the field of engineering. It serves as a platform to recognize the contributions of women engineers, highlight their achievements, and address the challenges they may face in their careers. This event aims to inspire and encourage women to pursue careers in engineering, promote diversity and inclusion within the engineering profession, and foster a supportive community where women can thrive and excel.

i. Young Professional (YP)

The "Young Professional" event provides a platform for early-career individuals to gain practical advice, expand their professional networks, and access resources that can accelerate their career growth. By engaging with experienced professionals, sharing experiences with peers, and participating in interactive sessions, attendees are empowered to take ownership of their career development and make informed decisions about their professional future.

About InGRASS

InGARSS stands for the Indian Geoscience and Remote Sensing Symposium. It is an annual conference organized by the IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Society (GRSS) dedicated to showcasing the latest advancements in remote sensing technologies and applications.
